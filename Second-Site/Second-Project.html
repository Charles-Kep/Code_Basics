Dr. Marie Curie

Pioneer in the research in radioactivity

Timeline of Marie Curie's Life

  • 1867 - born in Warsaw, in Congress Poland in the Russian Empire
  • 1891 - she left Poland for France and enrolled at the University of Paris
  • 1893 - she was awarded a degree in physics and began work in an industrial laboratory of Gabriel Lippmann
  • 1894 - Earned a second degree
  • 1895 - she marries Pierre Curie
  • 1898 - she and her husband published a joint paper announcing the existence of an element they named "polonium", later that year announced the existence of a second element, which they named "radium"
  • 1900 - she became the first woman faculty member at the École Normale Supérieure
  • 1903 - she was awarded her doctorate from the University of Paris
  • 1903 the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences awarded Pierre Curie, Marie Curie, and Henri Becquerel the Nobel Prize in Physics
  • 1911 - recieves second Nobel Prize in Chemistry
  • 1934 - dies at the age of 66
